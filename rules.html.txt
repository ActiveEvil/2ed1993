Damage
<p class="text-lg">
    When a weapon scores a hit, a check is made to see if it will wound the target. The weapon&apos;s
    <strong>Strength</strong> characteristic is compared with the target&apos;s <strong>Toughness</strong>
    characteristic using the chart below. The number shown is the minimum score on a D6 needed to convert the hit into
    damage. If this roll succeeds, then the target receives damage according to the weapon&apos;s
    <strong>Damage</strong> characteristic.
</p>
<p class="text-lg">
    Some hits are simply not powerful enough to damage certain targets; in those cases, the hit automatically fails to
    wound.
</p>

Armour Saves
<p class="text-lg">
    There are numerous types of armor in the 41st millennium, ranging from the humble Flak jacket up to the almost
    impenetrable bulwark of Tactical Dreadnought Armor. If a model wearing armor is hit by a close combat or ranged
    weapon, an armor saving throw is made. If the model passes the save, no damage is taken. To pass an armor save, the
    dice roll must be greater than or equal to the <strong>Armor Save</strong> value.
</p>
<h3 class="font-subtitle text-2xl">Save Modifiers</h3>
<p class="text-lg">
    Some weapons are powerful enough to negate part, or even all, of an armor&apos;s protection; this is represented by
    a <strong>Save Modifier</strong> on the weapon&apos;s profile. For example, if a model is wearing Mesh armor with a
    base save value of 5+, and it is hit by a Boltgun that has a save modifier of -1, the modified roll becomes 6+. If
    the modified save is greater than the highest possible dice score, then the armour is automatically penetrated.
</p>
